There are several license types you can apply for depending on your situation:

  • Licensed Center - A provider who is licensed to provide care in a facility, other than a private home, for one or more children. To learn more about becoming a licensed center, visit this link.

  • Licensed Group Home - A provider who is licensed to care for between one and 12 children at one time in the provider's home. To learn about becoming a licensed group home, visit this link.

  • Licensed Family Home - A provider who is licensed to care for between one and six children at one time in the provider's home. To learn about becoming a licensed family home, visit this link.

After 29 months of continuous care, licensed group home providers can provide care to up to 14 children and licensed family home providers can provide care for up to seven children.
